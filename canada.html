Canada

Origins

Canada started off as a group of colonies, mainly from Britain and France. The five original colonies were British Columbia, Newfoundland, Nova Scortia, New Brunswick and Prince Edward Island, and the Province of Canada consisting of both Ontario and Quebec. Many of these colonies met together in conferences in order to collaborate with each other.

Founded

Eventually, these colonies decided to to come together and wrote a request called the British North American Act which was presented to the Parliament and the House of Lords in England. This act created Canada's Confederation on July 1, 1967, a day now celebrated as Canada Day.

National Anthem

National Animal

The beaver is symbolic of the fur trade that was prominent during Canada's colonial days.

Geography

Canada is the second largest country in the world. It has a large marine and coastal terrain, the largest coastline in the world in fact. The terrain itself consists of many forests, icey regions in the northern parts of the coutry, and some flat prairies. It also shares the Great Lakes with the U.S..

Culture

Canada is culturally diverse and does not have a single national culture. It is a mix of British, French, and even a bit of their neighbors downstair's, the U.S., cultures. It also have many influences from South Asia and has many distinct aboriginal cultures.

Interesting Facts

  • The national dish is poutine, consisting of fries, cheese curds, and gravy.
  • The U.S./Canadian border is the world's largest land border.
  • Canada is home to polar bears.
